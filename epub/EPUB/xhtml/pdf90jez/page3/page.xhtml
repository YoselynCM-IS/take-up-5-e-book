Presentation

 

The present course deals with a variety of interesting and novel topics which will allow you to

 

relate your English classes to other school subjects; this will enrich your world view and will most

 

certainly help you commit to your learning.

 

This book is based on the Common European Framework 
(CEF), B1 level.

 

Being able to master a language like English will give you access to the best academic and

 

professional possibilities.

 

Take up 
offers  
three modules 
in each level with  
five lessons 
each including  
Grammar and

 

Vocabulary sections 
, as well as the reinforcement of those  
with Reading, Speaking and Writing

 

activities 
. An important aspect of  
Take up 
is that before starting each module, students work in

 

a
socioemotional abilities 
activity which reinforces their own identity, and it also offers a cross-

 

curricular project to work on tranversality.

 

Take up  
offers  
strands of lessons (Grammar explanation, grammar practice, and practice on

 

the four abilities of language: reading, listening, speaking and writing) 
which equip students

 

with the necessary transferable skills beyond school. They allow students both, controlled and

 

freer language practice using what theyâ€™ve learnt in previous lessons in a cumulative way.

 

Students listen to a wide variety of  
realistic types of recording which include dialogues, and

 

reading texts 
. We also include  
pronunciation exercises 
of new words for students to feel more

 

confident when speaking.

 

Reading texts offer a wide range of genres. Students read the text a first time for general

 

information, and a second time to answer specific questions.

 

Students are given lots of opportunities to use new grammar in active oral communication. This

 

activity usually involves personalization. Students work individually, in pairs or small groups and

 

find out new things about their classmates. They are given help in planning and organizing the

 

information they need to use in their writing activities.

 

At the end of each unit, students present a project designed for them to integrate their knowledge.

 

Take up 
presents an assessment called  
Remember what you know 
at the beginning of each unit

 

so the teacher can measure previous knowledge.

 

Each module presents a  
Self-Assessment  
and a  
Reflection section 
so that students not only

 

check their progress in the language learning, but also their habits as students and a reflection

 

to improve them.

 

Take Up 
is made according to the requirements for learning a second language at a basic level,

 

at the end of each book, students have a  
Dossier 
containing a  
Workbook with extra activities

 

for each module, Lists of Regular and Irregular Verbs and Grammar References.

 

We hope you will enjoy using this book and we know you will become better and more confident

 

learners of English.
